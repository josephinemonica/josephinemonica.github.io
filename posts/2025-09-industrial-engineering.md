---
title: What industrial engineering actually is
date: 2025-09-22
---

When I tell people I work as an industrial engineer at a semiconductor company, the usual response is a polite nod and a change of subject. So here's what the job actually looks like.

An industrial engineer in a fab is, roughly, the person who makes sure the machines and the humans and the wafers all move through the building in a way that doesn't lose money. The tools are mostly statistics, queueing theory, and a lot of SQL.

The interesting part — the part I didn't expect — is how much of the work is *epistemic*. You are constantly deciding which measurements to trust, which correlations are real, and which "process improvements" are actually just regression to the mean dressed up in a slide deck.
