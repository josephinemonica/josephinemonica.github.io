title: From physics olympiads to robots
date: 2026-01-08

People sometimes ask how a physics background shows up in robotics work. The honest answer is: it shows up less in the math and more in the *habits*.

The habit physics trains is this — when something doesn't work, assume first that you've misunderstood the setup, not that the theory is wrong. In robotics this cashes out as: check your coordinate frames, check your units, check your timing assumptions. Three times. Then start doubting the algorithm.

The olympiad years also taught me to sit with a problem longer than is comfortable. A six-hour exam is a long time to stare at three questions. Most real research problems take much longer, but the shape is the same.

## What I'd do differently

If I were starting again, I'd spend less time on elegant analytical solutions and more time building small systems end-to-end. The lesson that compounds is *integration*, not derivation.
